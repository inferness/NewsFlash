Synology, a provider of NAS and data management solutions, has a powerful weapon against ransomware attacks

Michael Chang, Regional Sales Head, Asia Pacific of Synology, explains some of the data protection solutions they offer. First, Secure Sign In uses 2-factor authentication to prevent unauthorized access. Next, the Data Backup feature with Immutable Snapshot ensures that backed-up data cannot be deleted or modified by ransomware. Snapshot Replication is part of this solution.

In addition, if these two stages are breached, Synology has a 3-2-1 backup strategy. This means there are three backups of the data, stored on two different media, and one of them is stored off-site. With this, even if one branch of data is hit by a ransomware attack, there is at least one off-site backup that can be used for recovery. Synology provides various NAS solutions with this feature for small medium business (SMB) level and above with prices starting from Rp 5 million. Their proprietary operating system, Disk Station Manager (DSM), provides various tools and features to protect data.
