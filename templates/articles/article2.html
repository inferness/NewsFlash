Youtube's War Against Adblockers

5 minute read

Following YouTube’s global crackdown on ad blockers, users are resorting to more extreme measures to evade their new policy

The company started restricting ad blockers earlier this year as a “small experiment.” It later confirmed to The Verge that it’s expanding the initiative, hoping to motivate users to watch ads or cough up $14 monthly for YouTube Premium.

  • A Wired report found users are installing and uninstalling different ad blockers at a record rate as they try to find one that YouTube can’t detect.
  • AdGuard, one of the biggest players in the ad-blocking market, told Wired that it typically sees 6,000 daily uninstalls. But since the crackdown, it’s seen 11,000 on average, with a one-day high of 52,000 on October 18.

Youtube's decision to start refusing service to adblock users has sparked a strange situation where developers are now forced and even more motivated to come up with various method to by pass Youtube's adblock detection system. Resulting in more and more advanced adblockers that uses various creative methods to workaround Youtube's system. With some users even resorting to the extreme and trying out different web browsers such as Brave browser that has successfully allow users to continue using Youtube Adfree.
