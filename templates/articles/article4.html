Presidential Candidates and Their Political Gimmicks

Entering the political year, presidential and vice-presidential candidates are continually striving to increase their electability through various strategies, one of which involves political gimmicks.

Presidential candidate number two, Prabowo Subianto, is synonymous with the "gemoy" and "joget gemoy" gimmicks, where in several moments he was caught on camera dancing, which was then amusingly responded to by his supporters.

Meanwhile, the third-numbered candidate, Ganjar-Mahfud, has adopted the "salam tiga jari" (three-finger salute), which signifies "three fingers, three promises: obedience to God, compliance with the law, and loyalty to the people."

Not to be outdone, the first-numbered candidate, Anies-Muhaimin, has also implemented an anti-criticism gimmick, where they invite comedians or content creators to criticize them, showcasing their "anti-criticism" character.

Dedi Kurnia Syah, the Executive Director of Indonesia Political Opinion (IPO), believes that the political gimmicks employed by each presidential and vice-presidential candidate effectively boost votes, especially among the younger generation. He states that Jokowi is proof of the success of political gimmicks.
