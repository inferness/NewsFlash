The Xyloband wristbands from Coldplay's Jakarta concert on November 15 saw a 77% return rate

The Xyloband wristbands from Coldplay's Jakarta concert on November 15 saw a 77% return rate, directly confirmed by Image Dynamics representing PK Entertainment and TEM Presents, the concert promoters. This information counters earlier false rumors on social media suggesting a low return rate of 52%.

The promoters clarified that the accurate return rate is 77%, addressing the buzz around the initially rumored low return rate. Some concert attendees had chosen to keep their Xylobands despite requests from both the promoters and Coldplay's management to return them after the concert.

The Xyloband, known for its vibrant light displays synced with Coldplay's songs, has been a concert staple since the Mylo Xyloto tour. In the current Music of the Spheres World Tour, Coldplay has introduced more environmentally friendly Xylobands and reduced waste by not producing them for every concert.

Top return rates for Xylobands include Tokyo at 97%, Copenhagen at 96%, and Buenos Aires at 94%. These figures emphasize the success of the Jakarta concert in terms of Xyloband returns.
